Generate effective communication for various internal and external scenarios.

## Common First Step: Stakeholder Analysis

Before drafting ANY communication, analyze:

1. **Who is the recipient?** Role, decision-making power, what they care about
2. **What do they want to hear?** Map your request to their priorities
3. **What's the ask?** One clear, specific request
4. **What data supports it?** Numbers, timelines, customer names, revenue impact

## Upward Resource Request

For requesting SA support, specialist engagement, executive sponsorship, etc.

### Structure

```markdown
{Greeting — one line}

**背景**: {1-2 sentences: customer name, opportunity size, current stage}

**请求**: {specific ask: who/what/when}

**理由**:
1. {data point 1 — revenue/strategic value}
2. {data point 2 — competitive urgency}
3. {data point 3 — customer readiness}

**时间要求**: {deadline and why}

**预期产出**: {what this resource investment will produce}
```

### Rules
- Lead with the customer name and opportunity — not your need
- Quantify everything possible (revenue, timeline, competitive risk)
- One specific ask, not multiple requests
- Keep under 200 words for WeChat; full format for email

## Cross-Team Coordination

For aligning with BD, partner team, specialist SAs, or other internal teams.

### Structure

```markdown
{Greeting}

**共同目标**: {what we both want — frame as shared win}

**当前进展**: {brief status — what's already happened}

**需要配合**: {specific ask with clear scope}

**对你的价值**: {how this helps THEIR metrics/goals too}

**时间线**: {when you need it, when the customer needs it}
```

### Rules
- Frame as mutual benefit, not one-sided request
- Reference shared KPIs or goals
- Be specific about what you need and what you'll handle yourself
- WeChat: conversational tone, max 3 paragraphs
- Email: professional but warm

## Customer Email

For post-meeting follow-ups, proposal delivery, status updates to customers.

### Structure

```markdown
{Customer name} 您好，

{1 sentence: meeting reference or context}

{Main content: 2-3 paragraphs max}
- Key point 1
- Key point 2

**后续步骤**:
1. {action — who does what by when}
2. {action}

如有任何问题，请随时联系我。

{Sign-off}
{Name / Title}
```

### Rules
- Professional but not stiff — avoid corporate jargon
- Every email must have concrete "next steps" with owners and dates
- Keep under 300 words
- If attaching documents, summarize key points in the email body

## Weekly Update

For weekly customer progress reports to management.

### Structure

```markdown
# SA 周报 — {Name} — {Week of YYYY-MM-DD}

## 本周重点
- {highlight 1}
- {highlight 2}

## 客户进展

### {Customer 1}
- **阶段**: {stage}
- **本周动作**: {what happened}
- **下周计划**: {next steps}
- **风险/阻塞**: {if any}

### {Customer 2}
...

## 需要支持
- {support needed, if any}
```

### Rules
- Lead with highlights — what moved forward this week
- Each customer: stage + action + next step + risk (4 lines max)
- Flag blockers explicitly — don't bury them
- Keep total under 1 page
